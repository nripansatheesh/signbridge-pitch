Prototype Briefing

SIGNVOICE AI

REMOVING COMMUNICATION BARRIERS IN PUBLIC SPACES

THE VISION

SIGNVOICE IS MORE THAN AN APP; IT IS A BARRIER-FREE WORLD. WE AIM TO EMPOWER THE DEAF AND MUTE COMMUNITY TO COMMUNICATE EFFORTLESSLY IN HOSPITALS, BANKS, AND LOCAL SHOPS—RECLAIMING THEIR INDEPENDENCE THROUGH SEAMLESS, REAL-TIME AI.

HOW IT WORKS (DEEP DIVE)

1. Capture & tracking

Computer Vision (OpenCV) captures the live feed. MediaPipe extracts spatial coordinates of hand landmark points in real-time.

2. Gesture classification

A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) in TensorFlow analyzes the landmarks to identify and classify the sign.

3. Sign → Audio

The classified sign is instantly converted into spoken audio using native Text-to-Speech (TTS) libraries.

4. Audio → Sign

The shopkeeper’s spoken reply is converted to text (STT) and displayed as clear sign language animations.

ADVANTAGES

  • Instant independence in shops, banks, and schools.
  • Reduced social isolation and increased social inclusion.
  • Low-cost, mobile-deployed digital interpreter.
  • Learning Mode assists normal people in learning sign language.

PROJECT BLUEPRINT

Phase 1: Concept

UI/UX definition, hand tracking optimization, and concept vocabulary development (Malayalam context).

Phase 2: Product

Offline model integration, cross-platform mobile deployment using Flutter, and expanded gesture sets.

CHALLENGES & LIMITATIONS

Environmental dependency

COMPUTER VISION STRUGGLES IN LOW-LIGHT OR DUSTY ENVIRONMENTS, IMPACTING SIGN RECOGNITION ACCURACY.

Dialect variance

SIGN LANGUAGE IN INDIA (ISL) VARIES REGIONALLY, REQUIRING LOCALIZED VOCABULARY DATASETS FOR TRAINING.
