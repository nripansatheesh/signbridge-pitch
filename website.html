SignBridge AI

Bridging the communication gap between deaf individuals and hearing communities through real-time, edge-processed computer vision.

🎯 Why This Project?

Everyday transactional interactions are currently frictionless for hearing individuals, but present significant barriers for the deaf and mute community.

Existing translation tools rely heavily on typing, which is slow and removes the natural flow of face-to-face conversation. We need a solution that translates gestures instantly, just like spoken language.

⚙️ How It Works

SignBridge utilizes the device's native camera to track hand and body geometry in real-time.

  • Capture: OpenCV processes the live video feed.
  • Track: Google MediaPipe maps spatial coordinates of the hands.
  • Translate: A trained Neural Network classifies the gesture.
  • Output: Text-to-Speech vocalizes the request.
Python FastAPI TensorFlow MediaPipe

🚀 Key Advantages

  • Offline Edge AI: Runs entirely on local device hardware. No internet required for basic shopping vocabulary, solving connectivity issues.
  • Multilingual Output: Can process gestures and output audio in regional languages.
  • Context-Aware: Predicts the rest of the sentence based on the first gesture to speed up communication.

⚠️ Current Limitations

  • Lighting Dependent: Computer vision currently struggles in low-light environments.
  • Hardware Demands: Continuous video processing drains mobile battery quickly.
  • Dialect Variance: Sign language varies heavily by region, requiring localized datasets for training.
